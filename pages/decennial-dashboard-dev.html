Title: Decennial Dashboard

Date Published: August 24, 2021
Share Facebook Twitter Email

Accurate decennial census data are critical to effective decision making at the national, state, and local levels. The Census Bureau’s population counts affect Congressional redistricting, social program funding, school planning, and more. To help state and local decision makers and data users determine the quality of 2020 Census data, we developed this dashboard to analyze whether the Census data look reasonable relative to expectations. We will continue to update the dashboard as the Census Bureau releases new 2020 data products.

About this Tool

The purpose of this tool is to engage local governments and non-profit organizations to enhance their understanding of data used to implement critical programs. Often state and local agencies view themselves as just consumers of census data who deliver services. Our intent is to assist those users in building statistical capacity and understanding that they can become partners in the process and improve the relationship between statistics and ground truth.

The Decennial Dashboard provides indicators of divergence between the Census Bureau’s population estimates (with minor adaptations) and the decennial census for April 1, 2020. Results are shown for population counts, housing unit counts, group quarters, and Race and Ethnicity. Reports can be downloaded as Excel or .pdf files.
