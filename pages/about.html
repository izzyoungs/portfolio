Izzy (she/her) is a Masters student in Urban and Regional Planning at Georgetown University. Her interests include data-driven political advocacy and urban analysis. She aspires to responsibly utilize data to contribute to policy solutions around affordable housing, climate resiliency, transportation equity, racial justice, and economic equality. She is passionate about curbing the use of technology for government surveillance while utilizing data and sociology to study and solve various issues in cities. She is interested in finding holistic, evidence-based policy solutions which increase economic and political power for marginalized communities.


Izzy also holds a Bachelors in Political Science and Communication Studies from the Unviersity of Nevada, Reno, and enjoys hiking, hot springs, rock climbing, roller skating, watching/reading scifi, scooting, and taking public transit.
